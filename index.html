VISHING

  • Definition: A form of phishing where attackers use phone calls or voice messages to trick victims into divulging sensitive information.
  • Method: Attackers impersonate trusted entities, such as banks, government agencies, or service providers.
  • Goal: To gather personal or financial information, such as credit card numbers, social security numbers, or account passwords.
  • Tactics: Often involves creating a sense of urgency, such as claiming there's a problem with an account or offering a prize or reward.

Be Aware Of:

  • Impersonation: Attackers often pose as trusted entities like banks, government officials, or service providers.
  • Urgency: Calls may create a sense of urgency, pressuring you to act quickly, such as claiming your account will be suspended.
  • Unsolicited Calls: Be wary of unsolicited calls requesting personal or financial information, even if they sound official.
  • Verification: Always verify the identity of the caller by contacting the organization directly using a known and trusted contact method.
  • Follow-Up Actions: If a call seems suspicious, hang up and contact the supposed organization directly through their official website or customer service number.
  • Scam Signals: Look for signs of a scam, such as threatening language, promises of rewards, or requests for immediate action.

Staying alert to these aspects can help protect you from falling victim to vishing scams.

AI DEEP FAKES

  • What They Are: AI deepfakes are digital media (like videos or audio) that use artificial intelligence to create realistic but fake content.
  • Risks:
  • Misinformation: Can spread false information by creating fake content of public figures or events.
  • Fraud: Might be used to deceive people or commit identity theft.
  • Privacy: Can invade privacy by making unauthorized or harmful content.

Some Tips:

  • Unsolicited Calls: Be wary of unsolicited calls requesting personal or financial information, even if they sound official.
  • Verify Source: Cross-check the media with reliable news sources or official channels to confirm its authenticity.
  • Follow-Up Actions: If a call seems suspicious, hang up and contact the supposed organization directly through their official website or customer service number.
  • Check Context: Assess whether the content makes sense in the given context and be cautious of media that seems out of place or sensational.
